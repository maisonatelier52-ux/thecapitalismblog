Legal

This page provides an overview of the legal and compliance matters relevant to The Capitalism Blog's publishing activities, reader use of content, permissions, complaints, and formal requests.

Informational use of The Capitalism Blog content

The Capitalism Blog publishes journalism, analysis, and explanatory content for informational purposes. While all content is produced in accordance with newsroom standards, it should not be considered legal, financial, tax, medical, or other professional advice tailored to an individual's circumstances.

When reporting on legal, regulatory, financial, or other specialized subjects, The Capitalism Blog strives to use accurate sourcing, appropriate context, and precise language. Readers should seek qualified professional advice when making decisions that may affect their personal or professional interests.

Copyright, quotation, and reuse

Readers may link to The Capitalism Blog content and may quote limited excerpts with appropriate attribution where permitted by applicable law. Republishing full articles, commercial redistribution, large-scale reproduction, scraping for republication, or systematic copying requires prior permission unless a separate license or legal exception applies.

If you wish to syndicate, reproduce, translate, archive for commercial purposes, or otherwise reuse substantial portions of The Capitalism Blog content, please contact the newsroom before proceeding.

Complaints about accuracy, rights, or fairness

If you believe a The Capitalism Blog article contains a material factual error, omits significant context, infringes legal rights, or raises another serious concern, please contact the newsroom promptly with the relevant URL, the specific content in question, the basis of your concern, and any supporting documentation available.

Different concerns may be reviewed under different newsroom procedures. Accuracy-related complaints may be handled through the corrections process, while copyright, privacy, or other rights-related matters may require separate review.

What to include in a formal request

  • The URL or headline of the content in question.
  • A clear description of the statement, image, video, or other material being challenged.
  • The factual, legal, or policy basis for the request, including supporting documentation where applicable.
  • Your name, organization if applicable, and a reliable method of contact for follow-up.
  • If acting on behalf of another individual or organization, a brief explanation of your authority to do so.

Removal, restriction, and update requests

The Capitalism Blog reviews legitimate requests for correction, clarification, updates, restricted access, or content removal. Submission of a request does not automatically result in the removal or depublication of accurate reporting.

Requests are evaluated based on editorial standards, the public record, applicable law, and the public interest. Depending on the circumstances, an appropriate response may include a correction, clarification, update note, or follow-up coverage rather than removal.

Links to third-party material

The Capitalism Blog may link to third-party websites, official records, social media platforms, public databases, and external documents to provide sourcing, context, or additional information for readers. These external resources are governed by their own terms, policies, and editorial practices.

Links to third-party sources do not necessarily constitute endorsement of all content, views, products, services, or policies presented on those sites.

Formal notices and requests

For legal notices, permissions inquiries, rights-related complaints, or other formal requests concerning published content, please contact the newsroom directly so the matter can be reviewed by the appropriate team.

As of the date of this policy, The Capitalism Blog manages public-facing legal, permissions, and policy-related correspondence through the newsroom contact addresses published on this website.
